## Design Context

### Users
New Bangbuatong Hospital staff complete an anonymous Thai satisfaction survey after orientation, mostly on phones. HR, head nurses, and internal administrators review the dashboard, export Excel files, and print an A4 report for quality improvement follow-up.

### Brand Personality
Premium, official, careful. The interface should feel like a polished internal hospital report: credible enough for leadership, calm enough for staff, and precise about anonymity and data handling.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a light, document-forward visual system inspired by formal hospital paperwork and executive summary packs, not a generic SaaS dashboard. The design may move beyond the current Esper-inspired style when it improves clarity and perceived quality. Avoid vague decorative styling, loud gradients, playful visuals, and anything that feels like a marketing landing page.

### Design Principles
- Preserve trust first: privacy, validation, and access states must be calm, clear, and unambiguous.
- Make the report feel printable from the start: strong hierarchy, controlled spacing, restrained borders, and credible tables.
- Use premium restraint: fewer visual effects, sharper typographic hierarchy, and deliberate accent color.
- Keep Thai reading comfort high: generous line-height, stable tap targets, and no cramped form controls.
- Do not paraphrase the Thai survey wording; form and report text must remain exact.
